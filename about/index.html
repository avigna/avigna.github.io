About

I am a DARK Fellow at the Niels Bohr Institute, affiliated to the University of Copenhagen.
My research focusses on the progenitors, evolution and fate of massive binary stars. Interacting binaries give rise to a wide variety of transients such as stellar mergers, X-ray binaries, pulsars, supernovae, gamma-ray bursts and gravitational-waves, to name a few. I use different numerical methods, such as detailed stellar evolution and rapid population synthesis, to understand the most relevant astrophysical processes at different phases. I am a member and developer of Team COMPAS, a software which simulates and studies massive binary evolution.
I earned my PhD from the a href="https://www.birmingham.ac.uk/research/gravitational-wave/index.aspx">Institute for Gravitational Wave Astronomy at the University of Birmingham (UK).
