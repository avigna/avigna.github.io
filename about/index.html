About

I am a DARK Fellow at the Niels Bohr Institute, affiliated to the University of Copenhagen.

My research focusses on the progenitors, evolution and fate of massive binary stars. Interacting binaries give rise to a wide variety of transients such as stellar mergers, X-ray binaries, pulsars, supernovae, gamma-ray bursts and gravitational-waves, to name a few. I use different numerical methods, such as detailed stellar evolution and rapid population synthesis, to understand the most relevant astrophysical processes at different phases. I am a member and developer of Team COMPAS, a software which simulates and studies massive binary evolution. My current research revolves around double neutron star systems and stellar mergers leading to (pulsational) pair-instability supernovae.

I earned my PhD from the Institute for Gravitational Wave Astronomy at the University of Birmingham in England. I spent the last year of my PhD as a visitor at the Monash Centre for Astrophysics in Melbourne, Australia, as part of the OzGrav collaboration. I earned my bachelor degree in theoretical physics from the Universidad Autónoma de San Luis Potosí in México.
